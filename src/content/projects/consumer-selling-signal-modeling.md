Various dealership clients were looking to build inventory during 2025 as tariff impacts were still unknown. They requested better insights into which consumers were most likely to be selling their vehicles (not including trade-ins) in order to better target their marketing efforts.

The result was an end-to-end machine learning pipeline to identify consumers likely to sell their vehicles. The project involved fusing and cleaning multiple data sources including web based activity, vehicle ownership, demographic, and Kelley Blue Book interaction data utilizing Dataiku as the main development tool. A CatBoost classification model was built that prioritized high-probability sellers and was successfully deployed to production using MLflow for model management and containerization.

This gave sales reps much better indication of which customers to target for inventory acquisition efforts, and allowed them to be more efficient with their time and resources during a critical period for inventory building.
