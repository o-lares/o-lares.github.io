This project analyzed consumer interactions on Autotrader across more than 1.8M Smart Search events and 10K Shopping Assistant chat sessions. I fused Langfuse traces with web clickstream data from Snowflake to understand how consumers searched, refined their intent, interacted with chat, and behaved before, during, and after Shopping Assistant sessions.

The work combined deterministic rule-based analysis with LLM-based sentiment classification, topic categorization, and semantic clustering using Claude Haiku 4.5. I compared tradeoffs in cost, speed, and nuance across approaches while surfacing actionable insights on user frustration drivers, search refinement patterns, and product optimization opportunities.

I also deployed an evaluator framework to classify conversations and track Shopping Assistant performance quality, replacing manual analyses with a scalable approach for identifying anomalous agent interactions.
